Planar Terrain Spawner

Jan 2023 - May 2023

Project Description:
This project was undertaken during my internship with the Institute for Infocomm Research. Its aim is to develop a real-time planar terrain spawner inside the PyBullet real-time simulation software. This feature would be useful for conducting research into the interaction of robot digital doubles with the digitally spawned terrains.

For this project, I conducted in-depth studies into various plane segmentation algorithms, such as RANSAC, Hough Transform, etc. I also implemented and experimented with various state-of-the-art algorithms such as PEAC, MPS, etc., to evaluate the performance and accuracy of each plane segmentation algorithm.

With PEAC as my plane segmentation algorithm basis, I obtained the plane equation from real-life sensor data. Then, I filtered out the points that lie outside of the plane and performed a convex hull calculation to obtain the edge polygon points. By projecting these points onto the ground surface, I performed mesh generation, successfully reconstructing the planar terrain inside the simulator.
